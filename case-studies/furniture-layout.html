Make-it-Home — Furniture Layout Optimisation

An automatic furniture placement system using simulated annealing to optimise spatial arrangements against a multi-objective cost function encoding clearance, traffic flow, focal point alignment, and room balance.

2024 Solo Developer ~5 weeks C++ · Simulated Annealing · Optimisation
Live Demo Source Code

Problem

Furniture placement is an NP-hard combinatorial optimisation problem. The search space is enormous (continuous positions and orientations for multiple items), and the objective is multi-criteria (access clearance, aesthetic balance, focal point proximity, traffic flow). Simulated annealing provides a principled metaheuristic that can escape local optima and find near-optimal placements.

Approach

1

Cost Function Design

Multi-term objective: (1) clearance penalty — items closer than 60cm to walls/each other are penalised, (2) traffic flow — penalty for items blocking direct paths between doorways, (3) focal alignment — reward for sofa/chairs facing the TV/fireplace, (4) balance — penalty for centre-of-mass offset from room centroid. Terms weighted and normalised to [0,1].

2

Simulated Annealing

Initial temperature set to accept ~80% of worse moves. Perturbation operator randomly translates or rotates one item per step. Cooling schedule: geometric decay (T × 0.995 per iteration) over 50,000 iterations. Reheat trigger if no improvement for 5,000 consecutive steps.

3

Constraint Handling

Hard constraints (items must be inside room, no overlap) enforced by repair: infeasible moves are rejected before energy evaluation, not penalised. This avoids distorting the energy landscape.

4

Visualisation

Top-down 2D OpenGL renderer shows room polygon, furniture footprints, cost history graph, and animated annealing progress. Colour-coded heatmap overlays show traffic flow density.

5

Comparison

Final SA layout compared against random placement and greedy (best-of-1000-random) baseline. SA consistently outperforms both on composite cost score.

Key Learnings

Hard constraint handling via repair (rejection) produces significantly better results than soft penalties — the energy landscape stays smooth.

Cooling rate is the most sensitive hyperparameter; too fast causes premature convergence, too slow is impractical.

Reheat triggers rescued the optimiser from local optima in ~30% of runs.
