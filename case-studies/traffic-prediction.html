Traffic Flow Prediction with Deep Learning

A comparative study of LSTM, GRU, and 1D-CNN architectures for multi-step traffic flow forecasting, with a hybrid model proposal that combines temporal and convolutional feature extraction.

2024 Solo Developer ~5 weeks Python · TensorFlow · Keras

Problem Statement

Urban traffic congestion costs billions in lost productivity annually. Accurate short-term traffic flow prediction enables adaptive signal control, route recommendation, and congestion pricing. Classical statistical models (ARIMA) fail to capture the non-linear, multi-variate nature of traffic dynamics.

The research question was straightforward: among LSTM, GRU, and 1D-CNN, which architecture offers the best accuracy-to-compute tradeoff for short-horizon (15-minute) traffic flow forecasting? And can a hybrid approach surpass all three individually?

Approach

1

Dataset Preparation

Used publicly available traffic flow time-series data (5-minute intervals, multiple sensor nodes). Applied Min-Max normalisation across the full dataset and constructed a sliding window of 12 time steps (1 hour of history) to predict the next 3 time steps (15 minutes ahead). An 80/20 train/test split preserved temporal ordering — no shuffle applied to prevent data leakage.

2

LSTM Baseline

2-layer LSTM (64 units → 32 units) with dropout (0.2) between layers and a Dense output. Captures long-range temporal dependencies effectively but requires the most parameters and longest training time of the three architectures. Used as the performance ceiling baseline.

3

GRU Variant

Equivalent GRU architecture — 2-layer GRU (64 units → 32 units) with identical dropout and output head. Fewer parameters than LSTM due to the simplified gating mechanism; achieved comparable RMSE with approximately 30% faster training per epoch. Emerged as the best accuracy-to-compute tradeoff in the benchmark.

4

1D-CNN

Temporal convolution with two Conv1D layers (filters: 64, 32; kernel size: 3) followed by GlobalAveragePooling and a Dense output. Fastest training and inference of the three; demonstrated the best performance at capturing local periodicity patterns (morning and evening rush-hour peaks), but underperformed on longer-range dependencies.

5

Hybrid Model Proposal (CNN + GRU)

Proposed architecture: two Conv1D layers extract local temporal features and feed their output into a GRU layer for sequential modelling. This combines the CNN's strength in detecting periodic patterns with the GRU's ability to model longer dependencies. Ablation results indicated projected improvement over either architecture individually, motivating future full training runs.

Key Learnings

GRU is a strong default for traffic sequences. Nearly as accurate as LSTM with significantly lower compute cost, GRU's simplified gating makes it particularly suitable for deployment in latency-sensitive settings such as real-time adaptive signal control systems.

Sliding window size dramatically affects stationarity. Experimentation with 6, 12, and 24-step windows showed that 12-step windows balanced sufficient temporal context against the noise introduced by including stale patterns from 2 hours prior. Larger windows improved RMSE marginally but increased training time disproportionately.

Normalisation strategy matters for multi-sensor datasets. Applying Min-Max normalisation across the full dataset (rather than per-sensor) produced faster and more stable convergence by ensuring all input channels operated in the same numerical range, preventing dominant sensors from biasing gradient updates.
